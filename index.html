§ 02 — THE WAR OF WORDS
44 DAYS.
10 TRACKS.
HIP-HOP CHANGED.

From March 22 to May 5, 2024, a rapid-fire exchange rewrote the rules of diss culture forever. Critical moments are highlighted below.

MAR 22
Future, Metro Boomin ft. Kendrick Lamar
"Like That"
The opening salvo. Kendrick dismantles the "big three" in a single verse: "It's just big me." The feud ignites.
APR 30
Kendrick Lamar
"Euphoria"
A six-minute surgical strike. Lamar dismantles Drake's authenticity, questions his racial identity, and challenges his role as a father. No bars wasted.
Drake
"Family Matters"
Drake's strongest offensive: targeting Lamar, Rick Ross, Future, and The Weeknd simultaneously. Scorched earth strategy alleging domestic abuse and infidelity.
MAY 3
MAY 3
Kendrick Lamar
"Meet the Grahams"
Released minutes after "Family Matters". An open letter to Drake's family. The trap springs shut, proving Lamar had pre-written the response anticipating Drake's angles.
MAY 4
★ THE KILLING BLOW
"Not Like Us"
The anthem. Upbeat, infectious, devastating. Lamar's first solo #1 on the Billboard Hot 100. Five Grammys. Played at the Super Bowl to 133 million viewers. Song of summer, song of the century.

Not Like Us
Kendrick Lamar — May 4, 2024
"Tryna strike a chord and it's probably A minor"
Double Entendre — Pedophilia Accusation
Widely considered the most devastating single bar in the feud. "A minor" = both a musical key and "a minor" (underage person). The wordplay is so precise, it became impossible to unhear.
"You a colonizer, Drake / Use these n---as for your wave, Drake"
Cultural Appropriation
Lamar reframes the entire battle as a cultural defense. By labeling Drake a "colonizer," he alienated Drake from the hip-hop communities that sustained his street credibility.
